\section{Introduction}
In the Riccati recursion, states (and dynamics lagrangian multipliers) are eliminated using the dynamics equation
\begin{equation}
    f(x_k, u_k) - x_{k+1} = 0
\end{equation}
of which the linearization that appears in the constraint jacobian is
\begin{equation}
    B_k u_k + A_k x_k - x_{k+1} = 0
\end{equation}

Controls (and stage-wise equality lagrnagian multipliers) are eliminated partially using the equality constraints by decomposing the stage-wise equality constraint jacobian, given by
\begin{equation}
    H_u = P_l T_l \begin{bmatrix}\bm{I}_\rho & \\ & \bm{0} \end{bmatrix} T_r P_r
\end{equation}

In the case of implicit integrators, the dynamics equation is given by
\begin{equation}
    f(x_k, u_k, x_{k+1}) = 0
\end{equation}
which can be implemented by introducing the controls $z_k \in \mathbb{R}^{n_x}$ and enforcing
\begin{align}
    z_k - x_{k+1} &= 0\\
    f(x_k, u_k, z_k) &= 0
\end{align}
which leads to a specific structure in the stage-wise equality constraint jacobian, since the original equality do not depend on $z_k$
\begin{align}
    H_u &=
    \begin{bmatrix}
        \nabla_{u_k} g_\mathrm{eq}(x_k, u_k) & \nabla_{z_k} g_\mathrm{eq}(x_k, u_k)\\
        \nabla_{u_k} f(x_k, u_k, z_k) & \nabla_{z_k} f(x_k, u_k, z_k)
    \end{bmatrix}
    =
    % \begin{bmatrix} M_1 & \\M_2 & M_3 \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=3]
        & n_u & n_z & \\
        & M_1 &     & n_g\\ 
        & M_2 & M_3 & n_f
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    \label{eq:Hu}
\end{align}
where $M_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_g \times n_u}$, $M_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_u}$, and $M_3 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_x}$.

Instead of a lower triangular structure, an upper triangulare structure for $H_u$ is also possible, in which case we write
\begin{align}
    H_u &=
    \begin{bmatrix}
        \nabla_{z_k} f(x_k, u_k, z_k) & \nabla_{u_k} f(x_k, u_k, z_k)\\
        \nabla_{z_k} g_\mathrm{eq}(x_k, u_k) & \nabla_{u_k} g_\mathrm{eq}(x_k, u_k)
    \end{bmatrix}
    =
    % \begin{bmatrix} M_1 & M_2\\ & M_3 \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=3]
        & n_z & n_u & \\
        & M_1 & M_2 & n_f\\ 
        &     & M_3 & n_g\\ 
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    \label{eq:Hu-upper}
\end{align}
where $M_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_z}$, $M_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_u}$, and $M_3 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_g \times n_u}$.

In some cases, constraints on $x_{k+1}$ are transferred to stage $k$ using the dynamics equations. If the definition of \eqref{eq:Hu} is used, that leads to an augmented matrix 
\begin{equation}
    H_u = 
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}
        \nabla_{u_k} g_\mathrm{eq}(x_k, u_k) & \nabla_{z_k} g_\mathrm{eq}(x_k, u_k)\\
        \nabla_{u_k} f(x_k, u_k, z_k) & \nabla_{z_k} f(x_k, u_k, z_k)\\
        % \Block{1-2}{\nabla_{x_{k+1}} g_\mathrm{eq}(x_{k+1}, u_{k+1}) \nabla_{u_{k+1}}f(x_k, u_k, z_k)}
        \Block{1-2}{H_{k+1} B_k}
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    =
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=3]
        & n_u & n_z & \\
        & M_1 &     & n_g\\ 
        & M_2 & M_3 & n_f\\ 
        & & M_4 & n_c
    \end{bNiceMatrix} \label{eq:Hu-carry}
\end{equation}
where $n_c$ is the number of constraints carried over from $k+1$. Note that this is still a lower triangular matrix. If instead the definition of \eqref{eq:Hu-upper} was used, the structure would now be broken. Additionally, because the dynamics are given by $x_{k+1} = z_k$, the constraints are transferred completely to the columns corresponding to $x_k$.

Figure \ref{fig:lu_time_spent} shows the percentage of computation time of the Ricatti recursion spent in computing the LU factorization of the matrix shown in \eqref{eq:Hu-carry} as a function of $n_x$, $n_u$, and $n_g$. Especially if $n_g$ and $n_u$ are large, a significant percentage of time is spent in the LU decomposition, motivating the need to perform this decomposition more efficiently. For small values of $n_x$, the decomposition is less significant.

\section{Blocked LU Decomposition with carry-over of constraints}
To decompose the matrix in \eqref{eq:Hu-carry}, we first write
\begin{align}
    P_1 M_1 Q_1 &= L_1 U_1
\end{align}
where
\begin{align}
    L_1 &= \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & \\ K_2 & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} \end{bmatrix} & 
    &\text{and} &
    U_1 &= \begin{bmatrix} V_1 & V_2 \\ & \bm{0}_{n_g-\rho \times n_u-\rho} \end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
Define 
\begin{align}
    \tilde{L} &\coloneqq \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & & \\ K_2 & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \end{bmatrix}
    & &\text{such that} &
    \tilde{L}^{-1} &= \begin{bmatrix} K_1^{-1} & & \\ -K_2 K_1^{-1} & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
which leads to
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{L}^{-1} 
    \begin{bmatrix} P_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \end{bmatrix} 
    H_u
    \begin{bmatrix} Q_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_z} \end{bmatrix} 
    = \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=4]
        & \rho & n_u - \rho & n_z & \\
        & V_1 & V_2 & & \rho \\
        & &  & & n_g-\rho\\ 
        & (\tilde{M}_2)_1 & (\tilde{M}_2)_2 & M_3 & n_f\\ 
        & & & M_4 & n_c
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
\end{equation}
where $\begin{bmatrix} (\tilde{M}_2)_1 &(\tilde{M}_2)_2 \end{bmatrix} = M_2 Q_1$ with $(\tilde{M}_2)_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times \rho}$ and $(\tilde{M}_2)_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_z - \rho}$.

Continuing gaussian elimination, we can obtain
\begin{align}
    \hat{L} &\coloneqq \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & & & \\ K_2 & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} & & \\ K_3 & & \bm{I}_{n_f} & \\ & & & \bm{I}_{n_c}\end{bmatrix}
    & &\text{such that} &
    \hat{L}^{-1} &= \begin{bmatrix} K_1^{-1} & & &\\ -K_2 K_1^{-1} & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} & &\\ -K_3 K_1^{-1} & & \bm{I}_{n_f}& \\ & & & \bm{I}_{n_c} \end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
using $K_3 \coloneqq (\tilde{M}_2)_1 V_1^{-1}$, which leads to
\begin{equation}
    \hat{L}^{-1} 
    \begin{bmatrix} P_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \end{bmatrix} 
    H_u
    \begin{bmatrix} Q_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_z} \end{bmatrix} 
    = \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=4]
        & \rho & n_u - \rho & n_z & \\
        & V_1 & V_2 & & \rho\\
        & &  & & n_g - \rho\\
        & & K_4 & M_3 & n_f\\
        & & & M_4 & n_c
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
\end{equation}
where $K_4 \coloneqq (\tilde{M}_2)_2 - K_3 V_2$. Because of the zero-row, we must perform row pivoting in order to continue the decomposition. Hence, define
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{P} \coloneqq \begin{bmatrix} \bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c}\\ & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} & \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}
leading to
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{P}
    \hat{L}^{-1}
    \tilde{P}^\top \tilde{P} 
    \begin{bmatrix} P_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \end{bmatrix} 
    H_u
    \begin{bmatrix} Q_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_z} \end{bmatrix} 
    = \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=4]
        & \rho & n_u - \rho & n_z & \\
        & V_1 & V_2 & & \rho\\
        & & K_4 & M_3 & n_f\\
        & & & M_4 & n_c\\
        & &  & & n_g-\rho\\
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
\end{equation}
using the fact that $\tilde{P}^\top \tilde{P} = \bm{I}_{n_f + n_c + n_g}$. Now, a remaining LU-decomposition should be performed on the matrix $\begin{bmatrix} K_4 & M_3\\ & M_4\end{bmatrix}$ such that
\begin{equation}
    P_2 \begin{bmatrix} K_4 & M_3\\ & M_4\end{bmatrix} Q_2 = L_2 U_2
\end{equation}
Note that this matrix also has structure that can be exploited!

Note also that the permutation matrices $P_2$ and $Q_2$ affect also $K_3$ and $V_2$, hence we define
\begin{align}
    \tilde{K}_3 &\coloneqq P_2 \begin{bmatrix} K_3\\ \bm{0} \end{bmatrix}\\
    \tilde{V}_2 &\coloneqq \begin{bmatrix} V_2 & \bm{0} \end{bmatrix} Q_2
\end{align}

Finally, the decomposition of is given by 
\begin{equation}
    P H_u Q = 
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col]
        & \rho & n_f + n_c & n_g-\rho\\ 
        \rho & K_1 & & \\
        n_f + n_c & \tilde{K}_3 & L_2 & \\ 
        n_g-\rho & K_2 &  & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho}
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=3]
        & \rho & n_u - \rho + n_z & \\
        & V_1 & \tilde{V}_2 & \rho \\
        & & U_2 & n_f + n_c\\
        & & \bm{0} & n_g - \rho
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    \label{eq:final_decomposition-carry}
\end{equation}
and 
\begin{align}
    P &= 
    \begin{bmatrix} \bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & P_2 & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix} \bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \\ & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} & \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix} P_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \end{bmatrix}\\
    Q &= \begin{bmatrix} Q_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_z}\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}\bm{I}_\rho &\\ & Q_2 \end{bmatrix}
\end{align}

\begin{algorithm}
    \caption{Blocked LU decomposition of lower triangular matrix $H_u$ with carry-over}
    \label{alg:blocked-lu-carry}
    \begin{algorithmic}
    \Require Matrix $H_u$ given by \eqref{eq:Hu-carry}
    \Ensure LU decomposition with complete pivoting $P H_u P = L U$
    \State Compute $P_1$, $Q_1$, $K_1$, $K_2$, $V_1$ and $V_2$ such that $P_1 M_1 Q_1 = \begin{bmatrix}K1 & \\ K_2 & \bm{I}\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} V_1 & V_2\\ & \bm{0}\end{bmatrix}$
    \State $\tilde{M}_2 \gets M_2 Q_1$
    \State $K_3 \gets (\tilde{M}_2)_1 V_1^{-1}$
    \State $K_4 \gets (\tilde{M}_2)_2 - K_3 V_2$
    \State Compute $P_2$, $Q_2$, $L_2$ and $U_2$ such that $P_2 \begin{bmatrix} K_4 & M_3\\ & M_4 \end{bmatrix} Q_2 = L_2 U_2$
    \State $\tilde{K}_3 \gets P_2 \begin{bmatrix} K_3\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$
    \State $\tilde{V}_2 \gets \begin{bmatrix} V_2 & 0 \end{bmatrix} Q_2$
    \State $L \gets \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & & \\ \tilde{K}_3 & L_2 & \\ K_2 &  & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} \end{bmatrix}$
    \State $U \gets \begin{bmatrix} V_1 & \tilde{V}_2\\ & U_2\\ & \end{bmatrix}$
    \State $P \gets \begin{bmatrix} \bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & P_2 & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \\ & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho} & \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} P_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_f+n_c} \end{bmatrix}$
    \State $Q \gets \begin{bmatrix} Q_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_z}\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}\bm{I}_\rho &\\ & Q_2 \end{bmatrix}$

    \end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}


\subsection{Verification of blocked LU decomposition}
To verify \eqref{eq:final_decomposition-carry}, compute both the left and right-hand side. The right-hand side is given by
\begin{equation}
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col]
        & \rho & n_f + n_c & n_g-\rho\\ 
        \rho & K_1 & & \\
        n_f + n_c & \tilde{K}_3 & L_2 & \\ 
        n_g-\rho & K_2 &  & \bm{I}_{n_g-\rho}
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=3]
        & \rho & n_u - \rho + n_z & \\
        & V_1 & \tilde{V}_2 & \rho \\
        & & U_2 & n_f + n_c\\
        & & \bm{0} & n_g - \rho
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    = 
    \begin{bNiceMatrix}[first-row, first-col, last-col=3]
        & \rho & n_u - \rho + n_z & \\
        & K_1 V_1 & K_1 \tilde{V}_2 & \rho \\
        & \tilde{K}_3 V_1 & \tilde{K}_3 \tilde{V}_2 + L_2 U_2 & n_f + n_c\\
        & K_2 V_1 & K_2 \tilde{V}_2 & n_u - \rho
    \end{bNiceMatrix}
    \label{eq:right-hand-side-carry}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{align}
    \tilde{K}_3 \tilde{V}_2 + L_2 U_2 &= \tilde{K}_3 \tilde{V}_2 + P_2 \begin{bmatrix} K_4 \\ M_3 \end{bmatrix} Q_2
    = \tilde{K}_3 \tilde{V}_2 + P_2 \begin{bmatrix} (\tilde{M}_2)_2 - K_2 K_3 \\ M_3 \end{bmatrix} Q_2\\
    &= \tilde{K}_3 \tilde{V}_2 + P_2 \begin{bmatrix} (\tilde{M}_2)_2 \\ M_3 \end{bmatrix} Q_2 - P_2 \begin{bmatrix} K_2 \\ \bm{0} \end{bmatrix} K_3 Q_2 = P_2 \begin{bmatrix} (\tilde{M}_2)_2 \\ M_3 \end{bmatrix} Q_2
\end{align}

The left-hand side can be using the definitions
\begin{align}
    X_1 \coloneqq \begin{bmatrix} P_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_f + n_c} \end{bmatrix}
    H_u
    \begin{bmatrix} Q_1 & \\ & \bm{I}_{n_z}\end{bmatrix}
    &= \begin{bmatrix} P_1 M_1 Q_1 & P_1 M_2 \\ & M_3 \end{bmatrix}
     = \begin{bmatrix} L_1 U_1 & \tilde{M}_2 \\ & M_3 \end{bmatrix}\\
    &= \begin{bmatrix} K_1 V_1 & K_1 V_2 & (\tilde{M}_2)_1 \\ K_2 V_1 & K_2 V_2 & (\tilde{M}_2)_2 \\ & & M_3 \end{bmatrix}\\
    X_2 \coloneqq X_1 \begin{bmatrix} \bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_u} \\ & \bm{I}_{n_z-\rho} & \end{bmatrix}
    &= \begin{bmatrix} K_1 V_1 & (\tilde{M}_2)_1 & K_1 V_2 \\ K_2 V_1 & (\tilde{M}_2)_2 & K_2 V_2 \\ & M_3 & \end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
Such that we can compute
\begin{align}
    P H_u Q &= \begin{bmatrix} \bm{I}_\rho & \\ & P_2\end{bmatrix}
    X_2
    \begin{bmatrix}\bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & Q_2 & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_z-\rho} \end{bmatrix}\\
    &= \begin{bmatrix} K_1 V_1 & (\tilde{M}_2)_1 & K_1 V_2 \\ \tilde{K}_2 V_1 & P_2 \begin{bmatrix} (\tilde{M}_2)_2\\ M_3 \end{bmatrix} & \tilde{K}_2 V_2 \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}\bm{I}_\rho & & \\ & Q_2 & \\ & & \bm{I}_{n_z-\rho} \end{bmatrix}\\
    &= \begin{bmatrix} K_1 V_1 & K_1 V_3 & K_1 V_2 \\ \tilde{K}_2 V_1 & P_2 \begin{bmatrix} (\tilde{M}_2)_2\\ M_3 \end{bmatrix} Q_2 & \tilde{K}_2 V_2 \end{bmatrix}
    \label{eq:left-hand-side-carry}
\end{align}

This is caused by the fact that for small parameter values, the LU decomposition is not the dominant part of the computation time, hence the additional overhead of the implicit approach causes it to perform worse than the naive reformulation approach.

\section{Conclusion}
For some values of $n_x$, $n_u$ and $n_g$, the LU decomposition in the Ricatti recursion takes a significant percentage of the computation time. Hence, speeding it up can reduce the overall computation time. In the case of implicit integrators, the constraint jacobian (of which the LU decomposition is computed) has structural zeros which can be exploited.

Numerical results show that the time required to compute the decomposition can be reduced by 40-50\% by using either a blocked LU deomposition instead of a direct LU decomposition directly or by using a generalized implicit version of the Riccati recursion. 

If the computation time for the full Riccati recursion is considered, overhead in the implicit Riccati recursion causes it to perform significantly worse than the naive reformulation approach for small values of $n_x$, $n_u$ and $n_g$. However, the blocked LU decomposition only changes the LU decomposition and hence does not introduce overhead in the recursion, leading to better results. The overal computation time of the recursion can be reduced by up to 20\% by using the blocked LU decomposition approach.
